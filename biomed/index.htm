Type 1 Diabetes, unlike Type 2 Diabetes, occurs when a person's body cannot naturally produce enough insulin. This is because the Beta Cells that are located in Type 1 Diabetic's Pancreas are working correctly. Insulin is needed because it allows for the human body to use the sugar(glucose) that it is consumed as energy, this occurs because insulin goes into the cells insulin receptors telling the cell's to come to the cell membrane and take in sugar.

The diet for a Type 1 Diabetic varies from person to person as everyone is different. For a person with Type 1 Diabetes, keeping track of how many carbohydrates are in their food is essential. This information is essential as it can tell a person with Type 1 Diabetes how much insulin they may require. Protein and fat also affect this, however it does not have as rapid of an effect. Generally people with type 1 diabetes eat healthier food. Examples include egg whites, turkey bacon, and skim milk. More Diet info HERE

Type 1 Diabetes is manageable and people with Type 1 Diabetes can live long and healthy lives. However, people who have been diagnosed with Type 1 Diabetes will find that they must monitor their Blood Sugar Level very carefully. This is done by putting a bit of their blood on finger sticks and then having a machine analysis it. If their Blood Sugar Level becomes too high, they must inject themselves with Insulin in order to decrease their Blood Sugar Level. If a Type 1 Diabetic does not monitor his or her Blood Sugar Level, they risk become Hyperglycaemic.
